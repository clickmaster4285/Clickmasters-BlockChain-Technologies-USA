## H1: Blockchain Development Company Serving Providence — Brown University, Lifespan Health, and New England Manufacturing

**H2: Providence anchors the I-95 Boston-New York corridor's smallest state. Brown University's blockchain research, Lifespan Health's clinical systems, and Rhode Island's manufacturing base create the primary blockchain opportunities.**

---

**Brown University:** Brown's CS department has active blockchain research, and the Entrepreneurship Program has produced blockchain startups. The proximity to both Boston's and New York's blockchain ecosystems gives Providence-based companies easy access to talent from both markets.

**Lifespan Health System:** Rhode Island's largest private employer operates 6 hospitals and 30+ outpatient clinics. Healthcare blockchain — patient identity, pharmaceutical traceability, clinical trial data — is directly relevant to Lifespan's operations.

**Rhode Island Manufacturing:** The state has a dense manufacturing base in precision instruments, aerospace components, and defense systems. Raytheon Technologies (now RTX) has Rhode Island operations. Defense contractor supply chain blockchain for these manufacturers has natural addressable market.

**Small state advantage:** Rhode Island's size means relationships with state government decision-makers are more accessible than in large states. Government blockchain pilots (land records, benefits distribution) are easier to initiate with local government relationships.
