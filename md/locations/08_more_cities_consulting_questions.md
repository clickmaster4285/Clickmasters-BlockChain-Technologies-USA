## H1: Web3 Development Company vs Blockchain Consulting Firm — Choosing the Right Engagement Model

**URL:** /web3-development-company-vs-blockchain-consulting/
**Schema:** Article, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList
**Internal Links:** /blockchain-consulting/, /blockchain-development-services/, /how-to-choose-blockchain-development-company/

Before hiring any blockchain partner, it helps to understand the distinction between development firms (who build things) and consulting firms (who advise on things) — and which model your situation calls for.

### Development Company Engagement

A blockchain development company writes code, deploys smart contracts, builds frontends, and delivers production-ready systems. Engagements are typically project-based or retainer-based with defined deliverables.

**When to choose a development company:**
- You've completed discovery and know what you want to build
- You need actual code written, audited, and deployed
- You need ongoing development capacity beyond your internal team
- You want a single team responsible for end-to-end delivery

**What you get:** Deployed, audited, documented smart contracts; working frontend application; production infrastructure; post-launch support.

### Blockchain Consulting Engagement

A blockchain consulting firm analyzes your business problem, evaluates whether blockchain is appropriate, selects the right platform, and creates specifications for development — but typically doesn't do the actual development.

**When to choose a consulting firm:**
- You're still in discovery: "Should we use blockchain for this?"
- You need an independent audit of a blockchain strategy someone else proposed
- You're evaluating vendors and need an unbiased technical evaluation
- You need a business case document for internal approval

**What you get:** Strategic recommendations, platform selection analysis, technical specification, project plan, vendor selection criteria — but not production code.

### Clickmasters' Hybrid Approach

We combine both: our discovery/architecture engagement (typically 2-4 weeks, $15,000-$30,000) produces the strategic evaluation and technical specification; our development engagement then executes against that specification. Clients benefit from continuity — the same team that helped define the right solution then builds it — while maintaining the analytical rigor of a proper discovery process before committing to development costs.

## H1: 15 Questions to Ask Before Choosing a Blockchain Development Company

**URL:** /15-questions-before-hiring-blockchain-firm/
**Schema:** Article, BreadcrumbList
**Internal Links:** /how-to-choose-blockchain-development-company/, /how-to-negotiate-blockchain-development-contract/, /questions-to-ask-smart-contract-audit-firm/

**1. Can you show me deployed contracts you've written that are in production with real user funds?**
The most direct signal of actual production experience vs theoretical knowledge.

**2. What is your process when you discover a critical security issue mid-development?**
Strong teams have a clear protocol — they stop, document, communicate, and prioritize fixing before continuing.

**3. Who specifically will work on my project? Can I meet them?**
Avoid firms that show senior engineers in the pitch and assign juniors to execution.

**4. What audit firms do you recommend, and do you have existing relationships with them?**
Established relationships mean faster audit scheduling and smoother remediation processes.

**5. What is your test coverage minimum before you consider code "done"?**
Below 95% line coverage with invariant tests is a quality red flag.

**6. Have you ever had a contract you developed get exploited? If so, what happened?**
An honest answer here is more valuable than a perfect record that might be fabricated.

**7. Can you walk me through your deployment process step by step?**
Strong teams have documented deployment checklists, multi-sig transfer procedures, and post-deployment validation steps.

**8. What documentation do you deliver with completed code?**
Minimum: NatSpec comments, architecture diagram, deployment instructions, and external dependency documentation.

**9. What chains have you actually deployed to in production?**
Knowing the difference between having "worked with a chain" and having deployed production contracts is important.

**10. How do you handle scope changes mid-project?**
A well-run firm has a clear change order process with documented cost/timeline estimates before work begins on changes.

**11. What is your policy on code ownership and IP assignment?**
Custom-built contracts should be 100% yours. Any pre-existing libraries should be open-source (like OpenZeppelin).

**12. How do you stay current with new attack vectors and security research?**
Subscribing to Immunefi, reading Trail of Bits and rekt.news publications, following security researchers on social media are all signs of active security engagement.

**13. What is your communication cadence during projects?**
Weekly written updates at minimum; availability for urgent issues within a defined response time.

**14. Do you carry professional liability insurance?**
For contracts handling significant value: insurance is a reasonable expectation.

**15. What did you build that you're most proud of, and why?**
This tells you about their values as engineers — whether they prioritize security, architecture elegance, user experience, or throughput.
