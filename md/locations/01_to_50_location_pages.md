## New York–Specific Regulatory Considerations

**NYDFS BitLicense:** Required for any company engaged in "virtual currency business activity" with New York residents. The most demanding state crypto license in the US — requires dedicated compliance officer, cybersecurity program meeting NYDFS standards, consumer protection policies, and significant ongoing reporting. Our exchange and wallet builds for NY-facing businesses are designed to be BitLicense-ready from the architecture phase.

**NYDFS Part 200:** The regulation governing virtual currency businesses in New York. We design AML programs, cybersecurity frameworks, and operational policies that align with Part 200 requirements.

**New York Securities Law:** New York's Martin Act has broader application than federal securities law. Tokenized asset offerings accessible to New York residents require careful legal review under both the Martin Act and federal securities law.

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a BitLicense to operate a crypto business in New York?**
Any company engaged in receiving, storing, holding, or controlling virtual currency on behalf of New York residents must obtain a BitLicense from the NYDFS. This includes exchanges, custodial wallets, and some payment processors. Technical blockchain applications that do not touch user funds typically do not require a BitLicense.

**How long does NYDFS BitLicense approval take?**
The NYDFS application process typically takes 18–24 months. Several applicants have waited longer. Most businesses operating in New York either apply early or structure their service to not require a BitLicense until they are ready to handle the application process.

**Can you work with New York-based clients remotely?**
Yes. We have delivered projects for New York financial institutions, CRE firms, and fintech companies without requiring on-site presence. NDA is signed before the first call; all discovery can be conducted via secure video conferencing.
