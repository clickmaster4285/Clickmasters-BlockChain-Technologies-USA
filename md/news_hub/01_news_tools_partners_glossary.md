## H1: SEC Crypto Enforcement in 2025 — What US Blockchain Builders Must Know

**H2: The SEC has accelerated enforcement of unregistered securities offerings in the crypto space. Here is a plain-English summary of the key enforcement actions and what they mean for your project.**

---

## The SEC's Current Position

The SEC under current leadership has taken an aggressive posture on crypto securities enforcement. Key enforcement threads:

**Token issuance:** The SEC has consistently taken the position that most tokens issued to the public meet the Howey Test and are securities. Projects relying on the "utility token" label without fundamental substantive difference in the offering have faced enforcement.

**Exchange registration:** The SEC has taken the position that crypto exchanges trading securities must be registered as national securities exchanges. Several crypto exchange enforcement actions are in various stages of litigation.

**Staking products:** The SEC pursued enforcement against retail staking-as-a-service offerings from major exchanges, taking the position that these constitute unregistered securities.

---

## What This Means for US Builders

**Token issuance:** If your token has any investment contract characteristics (investors expect profits from others' efforts), engage securities counsel before any public announcement or sale. "Utility" labeling does not create legal protection if the substance meets the Howey Test.

**DeFi protocols:** The SEC has subpoenaed DeFi developers and explored whether protocol developers are operating unregistered broker-dealers or securities exchanges. If your DeFi protocol facilitates trading of tokens that may be securities: legal counsel is required.

**Tokenization:** SEC-compliant tokenization (Regulation D, A+, CF) remains available. The enforcement emphasis has increased focus on unregistered offerings — properly registered tokenization is less affected.

---

## Proactive Steps

1. Securities counsel review before public token announcement
2. Regulation D filing within 15 days of first sale
3. KYC/AML compliance from day one
4. FinCEN MSB registration if applicable
5. Document all legal opinions in case of future review

## H1: Ethereum Pectra Upgrade — EIP-7702 and What It Means for Wallet Developers

**H2: Ethereum's Pectra upgrade introduces EIP-7702, which allows EOA wallets to temporarily behave like smart contract wallets — without migration. Here is what the upgrade changes and what builders should do.**

---

## EIP-7702: EOA Delegation

**What it does:** EIP-7702 allows an externally-owned account (EOA — standard MetaMask wallet) to point to a smart contract implementation for a single transaction. The EOA can temporarily "become" a smart contract wallet, execute batch transactions, sponsor gas, and other ERC-4337-like features — without permanently migrating to a new account.

**Why it matters for wallet developers:** Users do not need to migrate from their existing EOA wallet to a new smart contract wallet to access account abstraction features. The 500M+ existing Ethereum EOA wallets can access AA features without migration friction.

**Developer implications:**
- Smart contract wallets (ERC-4337) and EOAs will increasingly converge in feature set
- Wallet applications should support EIP-7702 delegation for existing users
- The distinction between "regular wallet" and "smart wallet" becomes less meaningful

---

## Implementation Consideration

For wallet developers: support EIP-7702 in addition to ERC-4337. For dApp developers: both wallet types will increasingly support the same features (batch transactions, sponsored gas, session keys) — design for the features, not the wallet type.

## H1: US Stablecoin Legislation — GENIUS Act and What It Means for Businesses Using USDC

**H2: The US Congress is advancing stablecoin legislation. The GENIUS Act would create a federal regulatory framework for payment stablecoins. Here is what it would change and what businesses using USDC should know.**

---

## The GENIUS Act (Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for US Stablecoins)

The GENIUS Act would: establish a federal framework for "payment stablecoins," require 1:1 reserve backing (US dollars, Treasury bills, or Fed deposits), mandate monthly reserve attestations by registered public accounting firms, and create a licensing pathway through either the OCC (national license) or state banking regulators.

**Impact on USDC:** Circle, which issues USDC, already largely complies with the GENIUS Act's proposed requirements. USDC is 1:1 reserve-backed, attested monthly by Grant Thornton, and Circle has applied for a national bank charter. Passage of the GENIUS Act would formalize USDC's regulatory status and potentially increase institutional adoption.

**Impact on Tether (USDT):** Tether's reserve transparency and offshore domicile (British Virgin Islands) may not meet GENIUS Act requirements for a US-accessible stablecoin. If passed, USDT may face restrictions for US transactions.

---

## What Businesses Should Do

For businesses that have deferred crypto payment adoption pending regulatory clarity: the GENIUS Act progress provides that clarity for USDC. Payment infrastructure using USDC that complies with AML requirements should be low regulatory risk under any likely final legislation.

## What Is a Smart Contract Specification?

A smart contract specification is a plain-English document that defines, before any code is written:

- **State variables:** What data does the contract store? What are the valid ranges and data types?
- **Functions:** What does each function do? Who can call it? What checks does it perform before executing? What state does it change?
- **Events:** What events does the contract emit and when?
- **Access control:** Which addresses or roles can call which functions?
- **Invariants:** What must always be true regardless of what inputs are provided?
- **Edge cases:** What happens in boundary conditions?

---

## Specification Template by Contract Type

### ERC-20 Token Specification Template

```
TOKEN NAME: [Name]
TOKEN SYMBOL: [Symbol]
DECIMALS: 18 (standard)
MAXIMUM SUPPLY: [Number]

FUNCTIONS:
- mint(address to, uint256 amount)
  - Who can call: Owner only
  - Check: totalSupply + amount ≤ MAX_SUPPLY
  - Effect: Increases to's balance by amount, increases totalSupply by amount
  - Events: emits Transfer(address(0), to, amount)

- transfer(address to, uint256 amount)
  - Who can call: Any address
  - Checks: msg.sender balance ≥ amount, to != address(0)
  - Effect: Decreases msg.sender balance, increases to's balance
  - Events: emits Transfer(msg.sender, to, amount)

INVARIANTS:
- sum of all balances always equals totalSupply
- totalSupply never exceeds MAX_SUPPLY

ACCESS CONTROL:
- Owner: mint() only
- Everyone: transfer(), approve(), transferFrom()
```

### NFT Contract Specification Template

```
COLLECTION NAME: [Name]
SYMBOL: [Symbol]
MAX SUPPLY: [Number]
PHASES: [Allowlist / Public / None]
ALLOWLIST PRICE: [Price in ETH]
PUBLIC PRICE: [Price in ETH]
MAX PER WALLET: [Number]
ROYALTY: [Percent] to [address]
REVEAL: [Immediate / Delayed]

MINT FUNCTIONS:
- allowlistMint(uint256 quantity, bytes32[] proof)
  - Phase: ALLOWLIST only
  - Checks: Merkle proof valid, per-wallet limit not exceeded, max supply not exceeded, correct ETH sent
  - Effect: Mints quantity tokens to msg.sender
  
- publicMint(uint256 quantity)
  - Phase: PUBLIC only
  - Checks: per-wallet limit not exceeded, max supply not exceeded, correct ETH sent
  - Effect: Mints quantity tokens to msg.sender

INVARIANTS:
- totalSupply never exceeds MAX_SUPPLY
- Only allowlisted addresses can mint during ALLOWLIST phase
- Once MAX_SUPPLY is reached, no more minting
```

## H1: Blockchain Glossary — 50 Additional Terms for Developers and Business Leaders

---

**Airdrop:** Free distribution of tokens to existing wallet addresses, typically to reward early community members, incentivize engagement, or bootstrap token distribution. Major airdrops: Uniswap ($400 to all early users), ENS, Arbitrum, Optimism.

**AMM Invariant:** The mathematical formula that governs an AMM's pricing. Constant product: x·y=k. StableSwap: a more complex formula optimized for assets near parity. The invariant determines how price changes with trade size.

**AML/KYC:** The combined compliance requirement for crypto businesses — Know Your Customer (verify identity) and Anti-Money Laundering (monitor transactions for suspicious patterns). Required for any US business handling customer crypto funds.

**Atomic Swap:** A cross-chain transaction where two parties exchange assets on different blockchains simultaneously — neither party can receive without the other receiving. Uses hash time-locked contracts (HTLCs).

**Beacon Chain:** Ethereum's Proof of Stake coordination layer, launched December 2020. Managed the validator registry and consensus before the Merge. Now integrated into Ethereum's single PoS network.

**BFT (Byzantine Fault Tolerant):** A consensus property where the network continues operating correctly even if a fraction of participants (typically up to 1/3) are malicious or faulty. Hyperledger Fabric uses BFT ordering. Ethereum PoS provides economic BFT.

**Bond (Olympus DAO model):** A mechanism where users provide LP tokens or stablecoins to a protocol in exchange for discounted tokens vesting over 5 days. The protocol acquires liquidity directly (Protocol-Owned Liquidity) rather than renting it via emissions.

**Borrowing Rate:** In DeFi lending: the interest rate charged to borrowers. Determined by the interest rate model as a function of utilization. Updates continuously with each new block.

**bps (Basis Points):** One hundredth of a percent (0.01%). Used to express small percentages precisely. 1 bps = 0.01%, 100 bps = 1%, 10,000 bps = 100%.

**Bundler (ERC-4337):** A service that collects UserOperations from the alternative mempool and submits them as a bundle to the EntryPoint contract. Earns gas from the operations it bundles.

**CDP (Collateralized Debt Position):** MakerDAO's term for a user's position where they have deposited collateral (ETH, WBTC) and borrowed DAI against it. Now called "Vault" in MakerDAO's current terminology.

**Chainlink Functions:** A Chainlink product allowing smart contracts to call any external API and receive the result on-chain. Extends oracle capability beyond price feeds to arbitrary external data.

**Checkpointing:** In Ethereum PoS, a checkpoint occurs at the end of each epoch (32 slots). Checkpoints are the reference points for finality — once two consecutive checkpoints are finalized, blocks from before the first checkpoint cannot be reorganized.

**CID (Content Identifier):** The IPFS system for identifying content by its cryptographic hash. The same content always has the same CID — content-addressing rather than location-addressing. Format: Qm... (CIDv0) or bafy... (CIDv1).

**Composable DeFi:** DeFi protocols that can be combined (composed) — outputs of one protocol used as inputs to another in the same transaction. Uniswap LP tokens used as Aave collateral is composability. Often called "money legos."

**Concentrated Liquidity:** Uniswap V3's innovation where LPs specify a price range for their capital. Capital is more efficient (earns more fees per dollar) but suffers 100% impermanent loss if price leaves the range.

**Consensus Client:** In Ethereum's post-Merge architecture, the client responsible for PoS consensus (Prysm, Lighthouse, Teku, Nimbus). Works alongside an execution client (Geth, Erigon) to run a full Ethereum node.

**Counterparty Risk:** The risk that the other party in a transaction fails to fulfill their obligation. DeFi eliminates intermediary counterparty risk (no exchange can fail and take funds) but introduces smart contract counterparty risk (the contract code could be exploited).

**Create2:** An Ethereum opcode that deploys a contract at a deterministic address computed from the deployer address, a salt, and the init bytecode. Enables counterfactual deployment (computing the deployment address before deploying).

**Cross-Margining:** In derivatives: using a single collateral pool to support multiple positions simultaneously. Reduces capital requirements vs. isolated margining (separate collateral per position). Common in DeFi perpetuals.

**Dark Pool:** A private order book where large trades can execute without moving the public market price. Institutional crypto dark pools (Paradigm, Wintermute) allow block trades without public price impact.

**DEX Aggregator:** A protocol (1inch, ParaSwap) that routes trades across multiple DEXs to find the best price for the trade size. Splits large orders across multiple pools to minimize price impact.

**Deflationary Token:** A token where the total supply decreases over time — typically through a burn mechanism. Bitcoin is not deflationary (supply still growing until ~2140). BNB is deflationary (periodic burns reduce total supply).

**Depeg:** When a stablecoin's price deviates significantly from its $1 peg. USDC depegged to $0.87 briefly during the Silicon Valley Bank collapse in March 2023. UST depegged to near-zero in May 2022.

**Derivative (DeFi):** A financial instrument whose value is derived from an underlying asset. In DeFi: perpetual futures (derive from spot price), options (derive from expected future volatility), and synthetic assets (derive from oracle price of real-world assets).

**DEX Volume:** Total value of assets traded on a decentralized exchange over a period. Uniswap has surpassed $2 trillion in cumulative volume. Often used as a proxy for protocol health and fee revenue.

**Dilution:** Reduction in the percentage ownership of existing holders caused by issuance of new tokens. Staking rewards and liquidity mining are inflationary and cause dilution of non-staking holders. Non-dilutive distributions come from protocol fee revenue.

**Distributed Ledger:** A database maintained across multiple locations with no central administrator. Blockchain is a type of distributed ledger — the "distributed" means multiple parties maintain copies; the "ledger" means it records transactions; the "blockchain" means transactions are stored in cryptographically linked blocks.

**ETF (Bitcoin, Spot):** An exchange-traded fund holding actual Bitcoin (not futures). BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) launched in January 2024 with $15B+ AUM in the first month — the fastest ETF in history to reach $10B.

**Ethereum Improvement Proposal (EIP):** The formal process for proposing changes to Ethereum. EIPs go through draft → review → last call → final stages before being included in a network upgrade.

**Execution Client:** In Ethereum's post-Merge architecture, the client responsible for the EVM and transaction execution (Geth, Erigon, Nethermind). Works alongside a consensus client.

**Fee Tier (Uniswap V3):** The LP fee percentage charged per trade: 0.01% (stablecoins), 0.05% (correlated assets), 0.30% (standard), 1.00% (exotic pairs). LPs select their fee tier when providing liquidity.

**Finality (Economic):** The property where reverting a block would require burning more ETH than the attack would profit. Ethereum PoS provides economic finality after two checkpoints (~12.8 minutes). Not the same as mathematical finality (impossible to revert) — but economically equivalent for practical purposes.

**Flash Loan Fee:** The fee charged for flash loans. Aave charges 0.05% of the flash loan amount. At $1B flash loan: $500,000 fee. Flash loan fees are a significant revenue source for Aave.

**Floor Price:** The lowest listed price for any NFT in a collection on secondary market. Indicates the minimum market value any holder can instantly realize. Used as the primary collection health metric.

**Forking (Code):** Taking the open-source code of an existing blockchain or DeFi protocol and deploying it as a new project. Uniswap V2 has been forked hundreds of times (SushiSwap, PancakeSwap). Forking reduces development time but not audit cost — forks must be independently audited for their specific deployment.

**Frax (FRAX):** A fractional-algorithmic stablecoin from Frax Finance. Partially collateralized (USDC) and partially algorithmic (FXS token). Has maintained its peg through the 2022 market crash.

**Front-Running:** Inserting a transaction ahead of a known pending transaction to profit from the price movement the known transaction will cause. Pervasive in DeFi — MEV bots monitor the mempool for profitable front-running opportunities.

**Funding Rate (Perpetuals):** A periodic payment between long and short positions in a perpetual futures market. When more longs than shorts: longs pay shorts. Keeps perpetual price aligned with the underlying spot price.

**Gas Optimization:** Reducing the Ethereum gas consumed by a smart contract — lowering transaction costs for users. Techniques: packing storage variables, using calldata instead of memory, avoiding unnecessary state writes, using assembly for critical paths.

**Genesis Block:** The first block of a blockchain. Bitcoin's genesis block was mined by Satoshi Nakamoto on January 3, 2009, and contains the text "The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks."

**Governance Quorum:** The minimum percentage of governance token votes that must participate for a proposal to be valid. Too low: vulnerable to manipulation. Too high: gridlock. 4–6% of total supply is typical for active DeFi DAOs.

**Hard Fork vs Soft Fork:** Hard fork: backward-incompatible protocol change that splits the chain (all nodes must upgrade). Ethereum's The Merge was a hard fork. Soft fork: backward-compatible change — upgraded nodes enforce new rules, non-upgraded nodes continue to follow the compatible subset.

**Hot Wallet vs Cold Wallet:** Hot wallet: internet-connected, operationally active, holds a small percentage of exchange assets. Cold wallet: offline, holds the majority of exchange assets. The exchange standard: 2–5% hot, 95–98% cold.

**Impermanent Loss:** The difference between holding tokens in an AMM pool vs. holding them outside the pool. Named "impermanent" because it reverses if prices return to entry levels. Becomes permanent loss if the LP withdraws at a time of price divergence.

**IOU (In DeFi Context):** A token representing a claim on underlying assets. aTokens (Aave) are IOUs for deposited assets. cTokens (Compound) are IOUs for deposited assets. When you deposit USDC to Aave, you receive aUSDC — an IOU for your USDC + interest.

**Isolated Margin:** In derivatives trading, each position has its own separate collateral pool. Losses are limited to that position's collateral. Alternative: cross-margin (one shared pool for all positions).

**JIT (Just-In-Time) Liquidity:** In Uniswap V3, a strategy where liquidity providers add concentrated liquidity immediately before a large trade and remove it immediately after. The LP earns fees from the trade without bearing longer-term impermanent loss risk. Controversial — seen as extracting value from LPs who provide continuous liquidity.

**Keeper (DeFi):** An automated bot that performs protocol maintenance tasks: harvesting yield aggregator rewards, triggering liquidations in lending protocols, updating TWAP oracles. Keepers earn a fee for successful execution and compete to be first (MEV for maintenance operations).

**LBMA (London Bullion Market Association):** The standard-setting body for the global gold market. LBMA-approved vaults (like those used by PAXG) are considered the gold standard for physical gold custody.

**Ledger (Hardware Wallet):** A hardware wallet device that stores private keys in a secure element. "Ledger" is also a brand name (Ledger Nano S, Nano X). Widely used for individual cold storage of crypto assets.

**LIDO (stETH):** The largest liquid staking protocol for Ethereum. Users deposit ETH to Lido and receive stETH (staked ETH) — a token representing their staked ETH + accrued staking rewards. $30B+ TVL. The largest single source of staked ETH.
