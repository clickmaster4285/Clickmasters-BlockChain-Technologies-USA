## FAQ

**How does a blockchain credential work technically?**
The institution hashes the credential data (student name, degree, date, institution) and records the hash on a blockchain with the institution's cryptographic signature. The student receives a verifiable link or a QR code. An employer scans the QR, the verifier app checks the hash against the on-chain record — if it matches and the institution's signature is valid, the credential is authentic.

**Does the student's personal data go on the blockchain?**
No. The hash of the credential data goes on-chain — not the data itself. The hash cannot be reverse-engineered to reveal personal data. FERPA compliance is maintained by keeping the actual record off-chain and under institutional control.

**What does a credential issuance system cost?**
A blockchain credential issuance platform for a single institution: $40,000–$90,000. A multi-institution credential network: $120,000–$250,000.
